## 🧠 How The System Works

OpenFishh mimics the way fish move in a school. A large group of small agents works together to cover wide areas of the internet.

*   Discovery: Agents scan news feeds, social platforms, and archives to find new information.
*   Verification: The system checks every claim against provided evidence. It assigns a score to each source based on its quality.
*   Mapping: The software creates a visual graph of connections between facts. This shows how ideas relate to each other.
*   Reporting: You define a topic, and the system gathers a report based on the highest-scoring sources.

## 🛠️ Operating The Application

Once you open OpenFishh, you will see a simple control panel. 

### Setting Your Goals
Type your research question into the main box. Be specific. Instead of asking about cars, ask about electric car battery efficiency in cold weather. The agents perform better when you provide narrow topics.

### Managing The Swarm
The interface shows you how many agents work on your task in real time. You can adjust the swarm size using the sidebar. A larger swarm gathers more information but uses more of your computer memory. Keep this setting at medium for daily research tasks.

### Trust Levels
The software assigns a score to every piece of evidence. You can view the settings menu to change your trust threshold. A high threshold means the software ignores low-quality sources. A low threshold allows more sources but may include unverified claims.

## 📊 Viewing Your Research

The main screen contains the Belief Graph. This area displays your gathered information as small bubbles connected by lines. Click on any bubble to see the original article. The system highlights the specific sentences it used to verify the claim. You can save these graphs as images for your own documents.

## 💡 Best Practices

*   Run the application while your computer is plugged into power to ensure the agents complete long tasks.
*   Check the logs tab if the software seems slow. This area tells you if your internet connection is dropping packets.
*   Clear your cache once a month from the Settings menu. This keeps the database small and the software fast.
*   Use the search history feature to revisit topics you researched in the past.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if the internet goes down?
The software pauses automatically. It resumes the scan once it detects a stable connection. You do not lose your progress.

Does this software cost money?
No. OpenFishh is free to use.

Can I add my own websites?
Yes. Open the Sources menu. Paste the web address of your favorite news provider. The agents will prioritize these websites in future searches.

Why does the agent count fluctuate? 
Agents finish tasks and retire. The swarm replaces them as needed to keep the search area clean. 

Does this work on Apple computers? 
This version is designed for Windows. Future versions may support other platforms.

## 🛡️ Privacy And Safety

OpenFishh respects your privacy. All your research files stay on your local drive. The software acts as a browser to read pages. It does not share your identity or your search history. You remain the only user with access to your Belief Graph. The software updates automatically to fix bugs and improve performance. You can disable automatic updates in the Settings menu if you prefer to manage them manually.
